## Domain Value Mapping

| Domain | Technical Focus | Business Translation | The "So What?" |
|:-------|:---------------|:--------------------|:---------------|
| **A** | Data Coverage & Federation | The Asset Inventory | Do we actually have the data researchers want? Without this, the shop shelves are empty. |
| **B** | Semantics & Quality | Product Usability | Is the data messy or ready-to-use? High maturity slashes the time spent cleaning data. |
| **C** | Governance & Access | Speed to Market | How fast can we approve safe research? This is the primary friction point for industry. |
| **D** | Research Integration | Customer Success | Are people actually using the service? This measures real-world output, not theoretical capacity. |
| **E** | Public Trust | Social Licence | Will the public let us operate? Ensures we don't lose support through transparency gaps. |
| **F** | Sustainability | Business Model | Can we pay the bills? Moves us from short-term grant dependency to sustainable cost recovery. |
| **G** | Workforce & Culture | Talent Pipeline | Do we have the people to run this? Identifies critical skill gaps in engineering and AI. |
| **H** | Infrastructure | The Engine Room | Is our tech safe and scalable? Ensures we can handle AI workloads and cyber threats. |

---

## Why This Matters

The Business Value Map helps **non-technical stakeholders** understand what each domain means in practice. When presenting HDRL to boards, funders, or policy makers, the value translation provides:

- **Clear justification** for investment in specific domains
- **Prioritisation** based on business impact, not just technical maturity
- **Common language** between technical teams and decision makers
