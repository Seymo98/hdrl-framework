## Slide Contents

### 1. Moving from Fragmentation to Federation
The HDRL Framework as a bridge from inconsistent health data infrastructure to federated UK-wide capability.

### 2. The Challenge
Current health data infrastructure is inconsistent across nations and nodes — industry faces a "postcode lottery" of governance speed.

### 3. The Solution
HDRL as a scientifically derived roadmap, synthesised from 56 global frameworks. Rigorous, roadmap-based, and outcome-focused.

### 4. Bridging the Gap
HDRL fills the "missing middle" between high-level principles (Five Safes) and technical specifications (SATRE, ISO 27001).

### 5-6. Anatomy of Readiness
The 8 domains translated into business value, covering data assets, governance, research integration, trust, sustainability, workforce, and infrastructure.

### 7. Measuring Success
The 5-level maturity ladder, with Level 4 (Managed) as the target for UK Network collaboration.

### 8. Strategic Value
Benefits for policy makers, research services, and industry partners.

### 9. The Ask
Recommendation to adopt HDRL as the primary assessment and investment planning tool for the next 3-year cycle.
